Our Mission

The Mars Eden Initiative (MEI) is a global research and colonization program focused on building the first self-sustaining greenhouses and gardens on Mars. By pioneering new techniques in space agriculture, our mission is to ensure a viable food system for long-term human settlement.

Our Vision for Mars

Greenhouses on Mars will use hydroponic systems, solar power, water recycling, and Martian soil experiments to produce sustainable crops. Our team is designing sealed habitat gardens that will serve both nutritional and psychological needs of future colonists.

Who We’re Looking For

We are seeking visionary thinkers with technical expertise in the following areas:

  • Botany, Agronomy, or Environmental Biology
  • Aerospace Engineering or Systems Design
  • Astrobiology or Space Agriculture

Ideal applicants are graduate-level researchers or engineers (M.S. or PhD) with experience in space simulations, remote environments, or experimental agriculture. Spaceflight experience is a plus.

Apply to Join the Mission

Selected applicants will undergo a two-year Earth-based training program followed by deployment to Mars as part of a permanent colony. Your work will shape the future of planetary settlement.
